About Me

I am a mathematician. Currently, I am a research associate at the University of Glasgow. My mentor is Michael Wemyss. Before that I obtained my PhD at the Vrije Universiteit Brussel under the supervision of Michel Van den Bergh.

Broadly my interests are in algebraic geometry and categorical incarnations of this. My current research focusses on the interactions between commutative and noncommutative algebraic geometry, more precisely on noncommutative resolutions of various kinds with applications to noncommutative motives in mind. Lately, I have also been thinking about Brauer groups, delving into the realm of tensor triangular geometry and thinking about generation in triangulated categories.
